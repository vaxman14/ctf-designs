What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files that are stored on your device (computer, tablet, or mobile) when you visit a website. They allow the site to remember your actions and preferences over a period of time, so you don't have to re-enter them every time you return to the site.

Cookies can be "first-party" (set by the website you're visiting) or "third-party" (set by external services). They can also be "session cookies" (deleted when you close your browser) or "persistent cookies" (remain on your device until they expire or you delete them).

How to Manage & Disable Cookies

You have several options for managing cookies:

Browser Settings

Most browsers allow you to refuse cookies or delete existing ones. The method varies by browser:

  • Chrome: Settings → Privacy and security → Cookies and other site data
  • Firefox: Settings → Privacy & Security → Cookies and Site Data
  • Safari: Preferences → Privacy → Manage Website Data
  • Edge: Settings → Cookies and site permissions → Cookies and site data

Note that disabling all cookies may affect the functionality of this and other websites.

Our Cookie Banner

When you first visit our website, you will see a cookie consent banner at the bottom of the page. You can choose to accept cookies or dismiss the banner. Your choice is stored locally on your device.
