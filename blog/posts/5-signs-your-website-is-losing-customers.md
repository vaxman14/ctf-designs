Most small-business websites don't fail with a bang. They leak. A visitor lands, something feels off, and they're gone before you ever knew they were there. No error, no complaint, just a customer who quietly went to your competitor instead.

After building sites for small businesses, I see the same quiet leaks over and over. Here are five of them, and what to do about each.

## 1. It takes more than 3 seconds to load

Half your visitors are on a phone, on cell data, with zero patience. If your homepage takes four or five seconds to show up, a big chunk of people leave before they see a single word. Speed isn't a "nice to have." It's the front door.

**Fix it:** Compress your images (they're almost always the culprit), cut the heavy plugins, and test on an actual phone on cell data, not your office wifi.

## 2. The phone number isn't tappable

You'd be amazed how many local businesses put their phone number on the site as plain text. On a phone, a customer ready to call has to memorize it, switch apps, and type it in. Most won't bother.

**Fix it:** Make the number a tap-to-call link, and put it where a thumb can reach it without scrolling.

## 3. There's no obvious next step

A visitor should never have to wonder "okay, now what?" If your homepage doesn't make the one action you want them to take blindingly obvious, you've left it to chance.

**Fix it:** Pick one primary action. Call, book, or get a quote. Make it a button. Make it loud. Repeat it down the page.

## 4. It looks like it was built in 2012

Fair or not, people judge your business by your website in about half a second. An outdated design quietly tells visitors you might be outdated too, or worse, out of business.

**Fix it:** Clean type, real photos, breathing room. You don't need flashy. You need current and trustworthy.

## 5. You can't tell what's working

If you have no idea how many people visit, where they come from, or where they drop off, you're flying blind. You can't fix a leak you can't see.

**Fix it:** Add basic analytics. Even the free stuff tells you which pages people leave from, so you know where to look.

> The good news: every one of these is fixable, and most are quick. A site that loads fast, makes the next step obvious, and looks current will out-earn a prettier one that does none of those things.

If you read this and recognized your own site in two or three of these, that's normal, and it's worth a look. That's exactly the kind of thing we fix.
